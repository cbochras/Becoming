BECOMING — Project Bible

What This Document Is

This is the complete context for the game "Becoming." It contains the design philosophy, architecture, narrative direction, all key decisions made during development, and the current state of the codebase. A collaborating AI should read this to understand the project deeply — but should NOT treat it as a set of instructions. This is knowledge, not constraint.

Part 1: What Is Becoming?

A quiet, psyche-driven game where three internal voices shape who you are becoming — observed by a silent figure who is trying, imperfectly, to understand you.

It is not a decision tree. It is not a visual novel. It is not a walking simulator.

It is a meaning system that uses game mechanics as its form.

The player spends 15-20 minutes making choices. At the end, something will try — imperfectly, tenderly — to tell them who those choices made them. Not perfectly. Not completely. But honestly. And the attempt itself will move them.

The Three Pillars

The game was born from fusing three inspirations:

- Disco Elysium — Internal voices AS gameplay. Identity as a thing that shifts, degrades, and reforms. The psyche is not a backdrop — it IS the game system.

- Journey — Wordless emotional architecture. Proving that meaning doesn't require dialogue trees. The world itself communicates.

- Kentucky Route Zero — Poetic ambiguity. Choices that don't branch a plot but define a self. Architecture as metaphor.

How They Work

- Every player choice reinforces one voice and weakens another

- Voices have health states: Healthy → Weakened → Distorted

- Distorted voices corrupt their own philosophy (e.g., Curiosity becomes obsessive, Compassion becomes self-destructive, Stability becomes paralysis)

- Sub-expressions determine HOW a voice speaks — not just IF

- The same voice (Curiosity) sounds completely different in "exploration" mode vs. "confrontation" mode

The Psyche Leak (15%)

- 15% of the time, a voice speaks RAW — unfiltered by the resolver

- Like an intrusive thought breaking through resolved identity

- This prevents over-determination and keeps the system alive

- Creates moments of surprise for both the player and the developer

Part 3: The Archivist

The Design Evolution

The game originally had:

- A "memory seed" (golden square that grew with choices)

- A "Future Self" (separate entity that appeared at Night to speak)

These were replaced by The Archivist — a single unified entity that absorbs both roles.

Who The Archivist Is

The Archivist is NOT:

- An observer (too passive)

- A judge (too authoritative)

- A companion (too relational)

- A narrator (too omniscient)

- An NPC (too functional)

The Archivist IS:

- An unresolved self that is trying to understand the player

- A consequence of the player's choices

- A mystery that gradually becomes a mirror

- The embodiment of the Resolver's attempt to classify a human being

Confidence is not moral certainty. It is classification stability.

A player can be consistently contradictory and still produce high confidence — if the contradiction itself is stable.

The Three Dilemmas

Day 1 — The Memory: A memory of being understood by someone now gone. Open it? Hold it gently? Set it down?

Day 2 — The Path: A fork between something you've always wanted and someone who needs you. Walk toward want? Return to need? Wait?

Architecture Rules

- Resolver = classifier + aggregator only. Never generates text.

- Archivist consumes resolved state only. Never queries psyche/memory directly.

- Psyche leaks (15%) bypass resolver for voice lines — emergent surprise.

- Confidence never shown numerically — only through Archivist behavior.

- One source of truth per moment. All downstream reads from resolver.

Part 7: Design Philosophy

The Rules

- Never over-explain. The player should feel meaning, not read it.

- Never optimize. No scores, no progress bars, no "better" endings.

- Contradiction is human. The system holds paradox, doesn't resolve it.

- The Archivist is not omniscient. Their understanding is imperfect — like all understanding.

- Suggestive truth, not classified truth. The game suggests; never declares.

- Emergent > Deterministic. Psyche leak, confidence variance, sub-expression coloring create surprise within structure.

- The system should never fully agree about the player. A little contradiction is not a bug. It is the human part.

What This Game Is NOT

- NOT a personality quiz with a game skin

- NOT a branching narrative (there's one story, but it changes color)

- NOT a self-help app disguised as a game

- NOT an optimization puzzle

- NOT about getting the "right" ending

What Success Looks Like

A player finishes, sits quietly for a moment, and thinks: "That was... me. But also not me. But closer to me than I expected." And they want to play again — not to "win" differently, but to meet a different version of the Archivist.

Part 8: Key Design Decisions (and Why)

1. Why Three Voices, Not Two or Four?

Three creates a triangle — every choice reinforces one AND rejects another. Two is binary. Four is too diffuse for a 15-minute game. Three = tension + clarity.

2. Why Sub-Expressions?

So that "choosing Curiosity three times" doesn't produce the same person. Day 1 Curiosity (Exploration) vs. Day 3 Curiosity (Self-Inquiry) are different identities. The system recognizes depth, not just preference.

3. Why The Archivist Instead of a Seed?

The seed was symbolic but passive. The Archivist is relational. A seed growing is something you watch. An Archivist watching you is something you FEEL. The relationship creates the emotional impact.

4. Why Confidence Instead of Just Identity?

Because "who you became" is less interesting than "how knowable you were." A player who was consistent gets certainty. A player who contradicted themselves gets a confused, tender Archivist who TRIED to understand them. The attempt is more moving than the answer.

5. Why Night Phase for Archivist Interaction?

Night = vulnerability. It's when the player's guard is down (narratively). The Archivist approaches at the most intimate moment. Morning would feel clinical. Afternoon would compete with the dilemma. Night is where revelation lives.

6. Why 3 Days Not 7?

Scope discipline. Three days = three choices = one arc. The system produces 30+ unique ending experiences from just three decisions. Expand later only when each day adds genuine new mechanical possibility.

7. Why Psyche Leak?

Without it, the system becomes a perfectly obedient classifier. Voices only speak when "allowed." The leak makes them ALIVE — they break through, interrupt, remind the player that identity is not a clean process. This 15% is what makes the game feel human instead of algorithmic.

Part 12: Visual Direction

Aesthetic

- Minimalist but atmospheric

- Dark backgrounds (deep indigo #1a1e2e)

- Color as emotion (blue = curiosity, amber = compassion, green = stability)

- Abstract forms (silhouettes, glows, particles — not realistic characters)

- Space for projection (the less literal, the more the player fills in)

The Archivist Visually

- Abstract silhouette — gender-neutral, slightly ethereal

- Posture states: sitting → leaning → standing → offering

- Body responds to dominant voice (taller/rounder/heavier)

- Glow color matches psyche blend

- Confidence affects animation (smooth writing vs. hesitant scratching)

- Sway amount inversely proportional to confidence (uncertain = more movement)

Inspirations

- Journey's sand and light

- Gris's color-as-emotion

- Kentucky Route Zero's theatrical staging

- Limbo/Inside's silhouette storytelling

- Celeste's internal landscape made visible

- Poetry, not instructions

- Philosophical, not academic

- Intimate, not dramatic

- Quiet, not loud

- Suggestive, not declarative
