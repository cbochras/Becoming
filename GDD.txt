
# BECOMING — Game Design Document v3.0

## The Investigation Pivot

---

## 1. CORE IDENTITY

**Title:** BECOMING

**Genre:** Investigative narrative / Interpretation game

**Engine:** Godot 4.6+

**Platform:** PC (Windows/Mac)

**Art Style:** Painted 2.5D Minimal Realism

**Duration:** 45–60 minutes (first arc); expandable

**Developer:** Cherifa Bochras (solo dev, learning while building)

---

## 2. THE THESIS

> "How do humans construct truth from incomplete evidence?"

The player investigates. They gather fragments. They form theories. They commit to an interpretation. And they discover they were always incomplete.

The enemy is not evil. It's not a villain. It's not the system.

**The enemy is incomplete information. Just like life.**

---

## 3. WHAT THE PLAYER DOES (THE LOOP)

```
ARRIVE → EXPLORE → FIND → INSPECT → CONTRADICT → DECIDE → REGRET → REBUILD
```

Every location follows this:

| Phase | What Happens | Player Agency |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| ARRIVE | Enter a new space. Rain. Atmosphere. The Archivist is already there. | Look around. Take in the space. |
| EXPLORE | Move through the location freely. No forced order. | Click objects, approach things, observe. |
| FIND | Discover objects, notes, sounds, fragments. | Choose what to inspect first. Choose what to ignore. |
| INSPECT | Each object reveals a piece of a story. | Read, listen, examine closely. |
| CONTRADICT | Objects tell different versions of the same event. They can't all be true. Or can they? | Notice the conflicts. Form a theory. |
| DECIDE | The Archivist asks a question. The player commits to an interpretation. | Choose what you believe. Not what you "are." |
| REGRET | Later locations reveal: every object was true. Just incomplete. | Sit with the discomfort. |
| REBUILD | Adjust understanding. Carry forward. Repeat. | Re-enter with new eyes. |

---

## 4. WHAT THIS IS NOT

| It Is NOT | It IS |
|-----------|-------|
| A visual novel with choices | An investigation space with consequences |
| "Read → pick option → read result" | "Explore → piece together → commit → discover" |
| A psychology test with labels | A tragedy of incomplete information |
| Linear scenes with timers | Open exploration within contained spaces |
| Slideshow of images with text | Living places that react to attention |
| Identity quiz ("who are you?") | Interpretation game ("what do you believe happened?") |

---

## 5. THE ARCHIVIST

### Who They Are

The Archivist is not a narrator. Not a guide. Not a judge.

They are a **parallel investigator** — studying the same evidence as you, reaching their own conclusions. Sometimes different from yours.

### What They Do

- They're already in the space when you arrive (digging, measuring, reading)
- They don't acknowledge you immediately
- They react subtly to what you inspect (a head tilt, a crossed-out note, a step back)
- They never explain. They observe and record.
- Their confidence evolves based on what YOU do

### Visual Identity

- Not fully rendered. Silhouette with presence.
- Posture-based, not face-based.
- Painterly world, abstract character. This contrast is intentional.
- They are "a thought trying to become a body."

### Progression Across Locations

| Location 1 | Location 2 | Location 3 | Location 4+ |
|------------|------------|------------|-------------|
| Back turned. Working. | Closer. Notices you. | Mirrors your posture. Crosses things out. | Faces you. Speaks. Uncertain. |
| "Something is watching me." | "It's studying what I do." | "It's changing because of me." | "It doesn't know either." |

### Confidence System (Invisible)

The Archivist has **interpretive confidence** — not about the player, about their own understanding:

- **High confidence:** Smooth movements, decisive writing, clear posture
- **Low confidence:** Hesitation, crossed-out notes, averted gaze, trembling hand

This is driven by the **resolver** (see Section 9). The player never sees a number.

## 6. THE LOCATIONS (ARC 1)

### Primary Arc: Three Locations

| # | Location | Feeling | Core Question | Objects/Evidence |
|---|----------|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **The Greenhouse** | Warm, damp, overgrown, preserved. Like something cared for too long. | "What happened to the person who left these?" | Cassette tape, train ticket, photograph, buried box, disturbed soil |
| 2 | **The Flooded Train Station** | Beautiful, abandoned, impossible. Like departure became permanent. | "Did they leave — or were they left behind?" | Timetables, a bag, echoes, water damage, measurement marks |
| 3 | **The Empty Apartment** | Intimate, uncomfortable. Like somebody left five minutes ago — though they left years ago. | "Who decided this was over?" | Half-packed boxes, one mug, an unsent letter, a key on the counter |

|---|----------|---------|---------------|
| 4 | **The Observatory** | Vast, silent, mathematical. Like trying to measure something immeasurable. | "When did they know?" |

| 6+ | **TBD** | Grows from the story | Grows from the questions |

## 7. SCENE DESIGN (HOW A LOCATION WORKS)

### Every Location Contains:

- **3-5 inspectable objects** — each reveals a fragment of a story
- **The Archivist** — working in the background, doing their own investigation
- **Environmental details** — things you can look at but not interact with (rain, a light, a shadow)
- **One threshold moment** — after enough inspection, a question unlocks
- **One decision** — not "which are you?" but "what do you believe?"

### Investigation Rules:

1. **No forced order** — player explores freely
2. **Objects contradict each other** — the tape says one thing, the photo suggests another
3. **No object is "wrong"** — all are true, all are incomplete
4. **The Archivist is a secondary witness** — they react to what you find, but don't explain it
5. **The decision doesn't appear until you've investigated enough** — minimum 2-3 objects inspected

---

## 8. INTERACTION DESIGN

### How the Player Moves

**Point-and-click within contained spaces:**
- Fixed camera frame (one wide painting per location)
- Player clicks to inspect objects (hover → highlight → click → inspect)
- Objects react to proximity/hover (glow, shift, breathe)
- No character avatar necessary — the player IS the camera/cursor
- The Archivist notices where your attention goes

### Inspection Types

| Type | Example | What Player Gets |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **Listen** | Cassette tape | Audio fragment — a voice, a sentence |
| **Read** | Train ticket, letter | Text — specific details, dates, names |
| **Observe** | Photograph, scratched face | Visual — zoom in, notice detail |
| **Touch** | Box, mug, key | Haptic feedback — temperature, texture described |
| **Watch** | The Archivist working | Behavior — what they cross out, what they linger on |

### Decision Design

Decisions are NOT:
- "Which personality type are you?"
- "[1] Curiosity [2] Compassion [3] Stability"

Decisions ARE:
- "Which object do you believe tells the most truthful version?"
- "What do you think happened here?"
- "Who do you think left first?"

The player commits to an **interpretation**, not an identity.

## 9. SYSTEMS (INVISIBLE TO PLAYER)

### Psyche System (Still Running)

The three lenses still operate — but the player NEVER sees them:

| Lens | Triggered By | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Inquiry** | Inspecting everything, clicking the Archivist, studying contradictions | Player who examines all evidence before deciding |
| **Tenderness** | Lingering on emotional objects, touching things gently, choosing "care" interpretations | Player who picks the photograph, stays with the mug |
| **Stillness** | Waiting, observing without clicking, choosing "acceptance" interpretations | Player who watches the Archivist, leaves things untouched |

### Identity Resolver

Still the single source of truth. Resolves psyche state + memory + day + evidence gathered into a unified state that drives:
- Archivist confidence
- Which details are available in next location
- How the ending manifests

### Identity Memory

Records everything:
- What the player inspected (and in what order)
- What they ignored
- How long they lingered
- What they decided
- What the Archivist's confidence was at that moment

### Evidence System (NEW)

Tracks what the player has found:
```
evidence = {
    "greenhouse": {
        "tape_inspected": true,
        "tape_content": "If you're hearing this, the train has already left.",
        "ticket_inspected": true,
        "photo_inspected": false,
        "box_inspected": true,
        "dirt_inspected": false,
        "archivist_watched": true,
        "decision": "tape"
    }
}
```

This drives what's available in future locations.

## 10. ART DIRECTION

### Style: "Painted 2.5D Minimal Realism"

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Painted** | Textured, atmospheric, hand-crafted feeling. Not flat, not pixel. |
| **2.5D** | Layered depth with parallax. Foreground/midground/background at different speeds. |
| **Minimal** | Not cluttered. Deliberate emptiness. Space to breathe. Objects that matter stand out because nothing else competes. |
| **Realism** | Grounded in real places (greenhouse, station, apartment). Not fantasy. Not abstract. |

### Emotional Rules for Art

| Location | Must Feel Like |
|----------|---------------|
| Greenhouse | "Abandoned place still breathing" |
| Train Station | "Departure became permanent" |
| Apartment | "Someone left five minutes ago — but years ago" |

### Key Visual Principles

1. **Places feel real** — not fantasy realms, not dream dimensions
2. **Places feel slightly wrong** — not horror, just wrong enough, like memory
3. **Objects matter** — few objects, each clearly readable, each emotionally charged
4. **The Archivist remains visually separate** — world is detailed, Archivist is not
5. **Empty space is storytelling** — what's missing matters as much as what's there

### Production Pipeline

1. AI-generate base images (Adobe Firefly / Leonardo)
2. Refine in Krita/Canva — painterly touch-ups
3. Split into parallax layers (far, mid, near, foreground)
4. Import into Godot as layered sprites
5. Add particles (rain, dust, fog) + 2D lighting
6. Place interactive objects as Area2D nodes

## 11. AUDIO DESIGN

### Layers

| Layer | Purpose | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Ambient drone** | Emotional foundation per location | Greenhouse: warm hum + rain. Station: hollow echo + dripping. |
| **Environmental SFX** | Space feels alive | Rain on glass, wind through cracks, water dripping |
| **Interaction SFX** | Feedback for player actions | Click sound on inspect, tape hiss on play, paper rustle |
| **Archivist sounds** | Their presence | Pen scratching, page turning, breathing shift |
| **Music** | Sparse. Only at key moments. | A single note held during decisions. Silence during exploration. |

### Audio Rules

- **Exploration = ambient only.** No music pushing emotion.
- **Decision moments = near silence.** The weight comes from absence.
- **The Archivist's sounds are always slightly out of sync** — like they exist in adjacent time.
- **Audio dips on selection** — hum drops for 0.5s when player commits.

---

## 12. NARRATIVE PHILOSOPHY

### Core Rules

1. **Never label choices** — no "(Curiosity)" or "(Compassion)" visible to player
2. **Never resolve contradictions** — the game doesn't tell you who's right
3. **Every object is true** — all evidence is honest, all evidence is incomplete
4. **The player's interpretation IS the story** — there is no "correct" reading
5. **Playing again shows a different shape of the same loss** — not a "better" ending
6. **The Archivist is also incomplete** — they don't have the answer either
7. **Text is not UI** — it's memory leaking into the space
8. **Silence is gameplay** — lingering, waiting, not-clicking are meaningful actions

### What the Player Should Feel After Finishing

> "I did my best. And it still wasn't enough.
> And neither was any other option."

Not tragedy. Not sentimentality. **Recognition.**

The same feeling as finishing Outer Wilds.

## 13. TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

### Godot Scene Structure (Per Location)

```
LocationScene (Node2D)
├── Background (Sprite2D / ParallaxBackground)
├── AtmosphereFX (GPUParticles2D)
├── AmbientLight (DirectionalLight2D)
├── Archivist (Node2D)
│   ├── Sprite (Sprite2D)
│   └── PoseController (Node)
├── Inspectables (Node2D)
│   ├── Object1 (Area2D + Sprite2D + CollisionShape2D)
│   ├── Object2 (Area2D + Sprite2D + CollisionShape2D)
│   └── Object3 (Area2D + Sprite2D + CollisionShape2D)
├── UI (CanvasLayer)
│   ├── InspectionPanel (for close-up text/audio)
│   ├── InteractionText (Label)
│   └── Fade (ColorRect)
├── Audio (Node)
│   ├── AmbientLoop (AudioStreamPlayer)
│   ├── EnvironmentSFX (AudioStreamPlayer)
│   └── InteractionSFX (AudioStreamPlayer)
└── SceneController.gd
```

### Scene Manager (Global)

```
Main (Node)
├── SceneManager.gd (loads/unloads location scenes)
├── GameState.gd (autoload — evidence, psyche, memory)
├── ArchivistState.gd (autoload — confidence, observations)
└── AudioManager.gd (autoload — cross-scene ambient)
```

### Core Systems (Keep from Previous)

- `psyche_system.gd` — lens tracking (invisible)
- `voice.gd` — resource for lenses
- `identity_memory.gd` — records all player actions
- `identity_resolver.gd` — single source of truth
- `typewriter.gd` — text reveal for inspection text

### New Systems Needed

- `evidence_tracker.gd` — what the player has found
- `inspection_system.gd` — handles object close-ups
- `scene_manager.gd` — location transitions
- `archivist_behavior.gd` — pose/reaction state machine

## 14. SCOPE & TIMELINE

- Final art direction proven
- Investigation loop working
- Archivist reacting
- Audio layer complete

## 15. DESIGN PILLARS (Ranked by Priority)

1. **The player investigates** — they explore, they gather, they piece together
2. **Nothing is explained** — the system never reveals itself
3. **The Archivist is alive** — a parallel investigator, not a narrator
4. **Places feel remembered** — real locations, slightly wrong, like memory
5. **Incompleteness is the point** — every truth is partial, every choice costs something
6. **Silence is gameplay** — what you don't click matters as much as what you do

---

## 16. SUCCESS CRITERIA

The game is working when:

- A player explores the Greenhouse for 5+ minutes without being told to
- A player pauses before making a decision (not because they're confused — because they're uncertain)
- A player notices the Archivist reacting and feels watched
- A player finishes and says: "I want to play again but differently"
- A player realizes on the second playthrough that the "wrong" choice was also "right"

---

## 17. THE PROMISE

This game is not about who you are.

It's about how you build truth from fragments.

And the quiet devastation of discovering that every fragment was honest —

and still not enough.
